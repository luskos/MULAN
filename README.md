We present a fully thermodynamically consistent propellantless thrust concept based
on asymmetric nanoconfinement of polar molecules within a refractory plasmonic lattice.
The MULAN (Momentum Uplifting via Light Amplification in Nanoconfinement) v3 engine
replaces the mechanical ratchet cycle with an electronic triplet-state excitation scheme
that bypasses fast vibrational relaxation (1-2 ps). Spatial asymmetry is enforced via a
passive photonic nano-collet (metasurface), forcing directional photon emission. Momentum
conservation is preserved by directional emission, but a new dimensionless coefficient K =
pout/pin is introduced, allowing K > 1 via thermal assistance (hot reservoir) without violating
the second law of thermodynamics. Using ultra-high temperature carbides such as Ta4HfC5
(Tmelt ≈ 4200 K), we achieve K ≈ 15 theoretically. At realistic spacecraft power levels (30 W
from solar panels) and active areas above 0.13 m2, MULAN v3 outperforms conventional ion
thrusters in thrust magnitude, while offering zero propellant consumption and operational
lifetimes exceeding decades. Numerical scaling laws, thermal management via pulsed/hybrid
operation, and experimental test protocols are provided
